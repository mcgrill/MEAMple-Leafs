gamewin strategy

The gamewin strategy is centred on getting speed bot to the puck first. The thought behind this is that the bot that maintains the puck first and fast will ultimately be able to make the play and the goal. The speed bot will constantly seek the puck on the offencive side of the rink and make bank shots based on their coordinates. The other two bots will also be versatile and able to shoot, but will have more of a defencive or midfield role to play. The defence bots will be communicating with one another more frequently, and will potentially be assigned roles based on conditional statements (i.e. goalie and defender or defender and midfielder).
mechanical design

basics

  • Robust design – can take a hit
  • Modular – must be easy to disassemble and swap parts
  • Main driver circuit board and then multiple breakout boards for extra sensor and actuator circuits
  • Must have traction on the rink, either through high friction wheels or heavy weight
  • Easy to access M2 and reset button

bot specifics

(1) Speed Bot
  • Light, 9V driven - with easy access for switching during timeouts and half
  • 2 Pololu #1163 (73:1) motors, fast, low torque
  • Possible pitfalls: Will the bot be heavy enough to have traction on the rink? Unless there is intelligent play, or luck, this bot can be pushed around easily.
(2) Defencive Bots
  • Heavy, 2x4AA driven
  • 4 Pololu #1163 (73:1) motors, slow, high torque
  • Possible pitfalls: Can’t get back to the goal fast enough Get pushed aboot by a massive bot (American)
actuator & sensor selection

actuators


All three MEAMple Leaf players will be equipped with large capacitors connected to solenoids; this will allow us to discharge a large thrust of energy for “slapshots.” In old Canadian tradition, capacitors are charged before the beginning of the match. Using MOSFETs as an electronic switch, we can set up multiple capacitors for multiple shots to occur, eh?

Solenoids were chosen over a motor driven slapshot because the solenoid is more consistent and can provide a large impulse on command.

sensors


Each MEAMple Leaf player will be equipped with an mWii sensor to track its own location and orientation on the rink. In addition, the MEAMple Leafs will have 4-6 phototransistors positioned around their bases in order to track the location of the puck. The MEAMple Leafs will constantly communicate with one another using the mRF module, so that all three will draw from the same pool of knowledge. This “hive” mind approach will give accurate puck location for strategic maneuvering.

Phototransistors will also be used for detecting puck possession. By setting appropriate thresholds on the phototransistors ADC values, accurate distances can be read from the sensors.
